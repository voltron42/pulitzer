MONSTER MAKER

Monster Maker is a competative deck-building game for 2 - 4 players. Players play rival mad scientists each trying to build the most powerful army of monsters that they can.

How To Play

Monster Maker is a deck-building game: means that each player starts with a small deck of the same cards and take turns acquiring cards from a shared pool of unique cards to each add to their own deck. In Monster Maker, the cards are the parts that the players will use to create their monsters. At the end of the game, each player will score the monsters that they are able to build from their deck. The player with the highest score wins.

How to create a Monster

Each card is one of three types: Head, Torso, Feet. Each card type has a related stat: Brains, Brawn, Speed.

Set - Up

To set up the game, separate and distribute the cards as follows:

Laboratories

Each player is given a Laboratory card and a slider. This is an oversized card which is not played, but is instead used by the players to track their damage taken. Attach the slider to the highest point on the damage meter. Each Laboratory also has a special ability.

How To Play

On your turn, you may use whatever cards you have in your hand to acquire cards from the center row, or, if you have one card of each type, you may create a monster and attack one of your opponent's. You may also use the special abilities of a card in your hand. Your turn ends when you have used or discarded all the cards from your hand and deal yourself a new hand of 6 cards. The next player begins their turn by scrapping (placing face up on the Scrap pile -- see above) the card on the Conveyer furthest from the center deck, sliding all of the Conveyer cards down one space, and drawing a new card from the center deck to add to the Conveyer. Once the center deck and the conveyer are empty, the game is over, and the players will score their decks (see Scoring below).

How To Acquire

The cards in your hand act as currency to acquire cards from the center row, but each type (Brains, Brawn, Speed) can only be used to acquire cards of the same type. Total up the points in your hand for each stat; you have that much to spend to acquire. When you have chosen which cards you wish to acquire, place both the cards you've acquired and the cards you've spent in your discard pile. For Example:

  • You have one brains cards in your hand worth 2 and there is a card worth 2 brains in the center row, you may acquire it.
  • You have two brains cards in your hand; one worth 2 and another worth 3, and there is a card worth 5 brains in the center row, you may acquire it.
  • You have a brains card worth 7 in your hand and there are two brains cards in the center row, one worth 3 and one worth 4, you may acquire both of them.

How To Attack!

If you are going to attack, you cannot use the cards you are using for your monster to also acquire. Separate out the cards for your monster and alert the opponent you wish to attack of your intent.

Your opponent then has the opportunity to defend. To defend, the defending player must create at least one partial monster and at most one complete or two partial monsters. If the defending player cannot do this, they must scrap two cards from their hand and take two damage on their Laboratory. If the defending player chooses to not do this, they must scrap two cards from their hand and take three damage on their Laboratory.

The Attacking monster (as with any complete monster) has three stats: Brains, Brawn, Speed. To successfully defend, the defending player's monster(s) must at least tie at least two of the three stats. If the defending player succeeds, the defending monster(s) are discarded. If not, the defending monster(s) are scrapped, and the player's Laboratory takes damage, one point if the defending player's monster(s) lose on two stats, two points if the defending player's monster(s) lose on three. (see Combat Examples below)
